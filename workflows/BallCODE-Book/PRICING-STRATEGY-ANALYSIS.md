## üí∞ $5 Price Point Analysis

### ‚úÖ PROS of $5 Price:

**1. Accessibility**
- ‚úÖ Very affordable for parents
- ‚úÖ Students can buy with allowance/money
- ‚úÖ Low barrier to entry
- ‚úÖ Impulse purchase territory
- ‚úÖ Builds larger audience faster

**2. Volume Potential**
- ‚úÖ More sales at lower price = potentially more revenue
- ‚úÖ Easier to get first customers
- ‚úÖ Word-of-mouth spreads faster (affordable to share)
- ‚úÖ Can sell to multiple kids in family

**3. Market Positioning**
- ‚úÖ Competitive with:
   - Children's e-books ($3-10)
   - Educational apps ($5-15)
   - Single lesson courses ($5-20)
- ‚úÖ Feels like a "deal" vs. traditional courses ($50-200)

**4. Psychological Pricing**
- ‚úÖ Under $10 = "affordable" mental category
- ‚úÖ $5 = "Why not try it?" decision
- ‚úÖ Low risk for parents ("It's only $5")

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è CONS of $5 Price:

**1. Perceived Value**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Might seem "too cheap" = low quality perception
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Parents might question: "Is it worth it?"
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Could undervalue your work
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Hard to raise prices later

**2. Revenue Math**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è After Gumroad fees (10% + payment processing):
   - $5 ‚Üí You get ~$4.25 per sale
   - Need 100 sales = $425 revenue
   - Need 1,000 sales = $4,250 revenue
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Lower margin per sale

**3. Sustainability**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Hard to justify time investment at $5
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Need high volume to be sustainable
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Customer support costs same regardless of price

**4. Positioning**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Might position as "cheap" vs. "premium"
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Could attract price-sensitive customers (more support issues)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Harder to justify higher prices for future products

## üí° Recommendation: Tiered Pricing Strategy

### Option 1: Start at $5, Offer Bundle (RECOMMENDED)

**Individual Books:**
- Book 1: $5
- Book 2: $5
- Book 3: $5

**Bundle (All 3 Books):**
- Bundle: $12 (save $3, 20% discount)
- **This makes $5 feel like a deal, bundle feels like value**

**Why This Works:**
- ‚úÖ $5 gets people in the door
- ‚úÖ Bundle encourages buying all 3
- ‚úÖ Average order value = $12 (not $5)
- ‚úÖ Still accessible, but better revenue

## School-first licensing (recommended primary model)

Schools rarely want to buy individual $5 items as the core procurement model. The procurement-friendly structure is:

- **Pilot (time-boxed):** low/no cost ‚Üí proof + testimonials
- **Classroom annual license:** 1 teacher / ~35 students
- **School/site annual license:** whole building
- **District license:** custom
