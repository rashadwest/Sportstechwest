## ğŸ¯ WHAT YOU'LL TEACH (The Concepts)

### Book 1: Sequences
**Basketball:** Breaking the press (move, pass, shoot)  
**Coding:** Sequences of commands  
**Python:** `move_left()`, `pass_ball()`, `shoot()`

### Book 2: Conditionals
**Basketball:** Reading defense (if defender is here, do this)  
**Coding:** If/then decisions  
**Python:** `if defender_near: crossover()`

### Book 3: Loops
**Basketball:** Repeating moves (fake left 3 times)  
**Coding:** Repeating actions  
**Python:** `for i in range(3): fake_left()`

**Each book:** 6-8 weeks, one concept at a time.

## ğŸ’¡ COMMON QUESTIONS (Quick Answers)

### "Do I need to know Python?"
**No.** The blocks make it visual. You can learn alongside your students.

### "What if students finish early?"
They can try the Python code, create their own sequences, or help classmates.

### "What if students struggle?"
The game provides hints. Students can also work in pairs or small groups.

### "How do I grade this?"
Use the provided rubrics. Focus on effort and understanding, not perfection.

### "What if I don't know basketball?"
The stories explain everything. You don't need basketball knowledge.

### "How long does each book take?"
6-8 weeks, depending on your schedule. Flexible pacing.

## ğŸ’¬ TEACHER TIPS (From Experience)

### Tip 1: Start Simple
Don't try to teach everything at once. One concept per book.

### Tip 2: Let Students Explore
The game is designed for exploration. Let students try things.

### Tip 3: Pair Programming
Have students work in pairs. They learn from each other.

### Tip 4: Celebrate Mistakes
Debugging is part of coding. Celebrate when students fix errors.

### Tip 5: Connect to Real Life
Show students how coding is used in real basketball analytics.
