# Writing Guide - Book 1: The Foundation Block
## Picture Book Format (~30 words per page)

**Copyright © 2025 Rashad West. All Rights Reserved.**

---

## FORMAT OVERVIEW

**Format:** Picture Book  
**Words per page:** ~30 words  
**Total pages:** ~10-15 pages  
**Total words:** ~300-450 words  
**Structure:** Each page = 1 scene with ~30 words + illustration

---

## PAGE-BY-PAGE WRITING GUIDE

### PAGE 1: Title Page / Opening
**Words:** ~20-30  
**What to write:**
- Title: "The Foundation Block"
- Opening line that sets the scene
- Introduce the basketball situation

**PROMPT:**
"Write 1-2 sentences (20-30 words) opening with a full-court press. Example: 'The full-court press trapped Nova's team. They couldn't move forward. Every fancy move failed.'"

---

### PAGE 2: The Problem
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Show Nova trying fancy moves
- Show them failing
- Create the problem

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova trying crossovers and spins, but all failing. Show the frustration. Example: 'Nova tried crossovers. She tried spins. Nothing worked. The team was stuck.'"

---

### PAGE 3: Arc Detects
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Arc speaks
- Arc's line: "Foundation missing. Basic sequence not established."
- Nova's reaction

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Arc detects the problem. Use Arc's exact line: 'Foundation missing. Basic sequence not established.' Show Nova hearing this."

---

### PAGE 4: Coach's Wisdom
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Coach Circuit speaks
- Coach's line: "Start with the foundation. Everything else builds from there."
- Nova thinking

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Coach Circuit gives wisdom. Use Coach's exact line: 'Start with the foundation. Everything else builds from there.'"

---

### PAGE 5: Nova's Decision
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova realizes she needs to try something simpler
- She decides to try the pound dribble
- End of Act I feeling

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova realizing: 'Maybe I need to start simpler.' Show her deciding to try the pound dribble."

---

### PAGE 6: Practice Begins
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova practices the pound dribble
- Describe the simple, repetitive motion
- Show her doing it: "Pound. Pound. Pound."

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova practicing the pound dribble. Show the simple, repetitive motion: 'Pound. Pound. Pound.' Make it feel like discovery."

---

### PAGE 7: Pattern Recognition
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Arc sees the pattern
- Arc's line: "Pound. Pound. Pound. Repeat."
- Arc explains this is a sequence

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Arc recognizes the pattern. Use Arc's line: 'Pound. Pound. Pound. Repeat.' Show Arc explaining this is a sequence."

---

### PAGE 8: The Connection
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova makes the connection
- She sees it as a "block" she can repeat
- Her realization: "This is like a code block!"

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Nova makes the connection. Show her realizing the pound dribble is like 'Block 1' that can be repeated. Use her line: 'This is like a code block I can repeat!'"

---

### PAGE 9: Final Challenge
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Final seconds on the clock
- Last chance to break the press
- Nova knows what to do

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) setting up the final challenge. Show the clock, the urgency. Show Nova knowing she needs to use the foundation blocks."

---

### PAGE 10: Using the Sequence
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova uses the pound sequence
- Show her doing it: Block 1 repeated
- Show it working

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova using the pound sequence. Show her repeating Block 1: 'Pound. Pound. Pound. Advance.' Show it working."

---

### PAGE 11: Success!
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Team breaks the press!
- They advance past half-court
- Show the success and relief

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing the success. The team breaks the press, advances past half-court. Show the relief and celebration."

---

### PAGE 12: The Connection
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova reflects
- Her line: "The foundation works! Now I can build on it."
- The connection to coding

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova's reflection. Use her line: 'The foundation works! Now I can build on it.' Show the connection to coding."

---

### PAGE 13: Final Message
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Final message about foundations
- Her line: "Coding starts with simple blocks, just like dribbling."
- End with understanding

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) ending the story. Use Nova's line: 'Coding starts with simple blocks, just like dribbling.' End with her understanding that foundations enable everything."

## WRITING RULES FOR PICTURE BOOKS

### DO:
- ✅ Keep each page to ~30 words
- ✅ Make each page a complete thought/scene
- ✅ Use simple, clear language
- ✅ Let illustrations tell part of the story
- ✅ Use dialogue to break up text
- ✅ End each page with a moment or action
- ✅ Make it flow from page to page

### DON'T:
- ❌ Don't write more than 30 words per page
- ❌ Don't explain everything - let pictures help
- ❌ Don't use complex sentences
- ❌ Don't put too much on one page
- ❌ Don't forget each page needs to work alone AND flow to next

---

## PAGE STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

**For each page, write:**
1. **Action/Scene** (1-2 sentences)
2. **Dialogue or Thought** (1 sentence, if needed)
3. **Result/Moment** (1 sentence)

**Example:**
- Page 6: "Nova practiced the pound dribble. Pound. Pound. Pound. It was simple, but it worked."
