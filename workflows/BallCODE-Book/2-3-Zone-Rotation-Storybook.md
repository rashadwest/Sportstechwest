## Skill Pit-Stop: Understanding Rotations and Loops

### What is a Rotation?

Think of a rotation like a dance. When one person moves, everyone else adjusts their position to keep the dance balanced. In a 2-3 zone defense, when the ball moves, all five defenders adjust their positions to keep the defense balanced.

### The Defensive Tree System

Just like the dribble tree has numbered actions (1-7), the defensive tree has numbered movement directions (1-5):

**DEFEND 1:** Move up or down (↑ ↓) — vertical movement  
**DEFEND 2:** Move left or right (← →) — lateral movement  
**DEFEND 3:** Close out angled down (↓ ↘ ↓ ↙) — angled defensive movement toward shooter  
**DEFEND 4:** Close out angled down (↓ ↘ ↓ ↙) — similar angled movement  
**DEFEND 5:** Hands-up posture — defensive stance when you're in position

When you need to rotate, you choose the number that matches the direction you need to move!

### The 2-3 Zone Formation

A 2-3 zone has:
- **2 defenders at the top** (guards) — responsible for the top of the key and wings
- **3 defenders at the bottom** (forwards/center) — responsible for the paint and corners

### How Rotations Work

When the ball moves, each defender follows their defensive tree sequence:

1. **Ball at Top:** Top defenders pressure, bottom defenders stay in position
2. **Ball on Wing:** Top defender closes out, other top defender shifts, bottom defender moves up, center shifts to help
3. **Ball in Corner:** Top defender pressures, other top defender covers wing, bottom defender helps, center protects basket

### The Loop Pattern

In code, a rotation is like a loop:

```python
while ball_is_in_play:
    if ball_at_top:
        rotate_to_top_defense()
    elif ball_on_wing:
        rotate_to_wing_defense()
    elif ball_in_corner:
        rotate_to_corner_defense()
```

Each time the ball moves, the loop repeats. Each defender executes their defensive tree sequence. And the defense stays balanced.

### Key Takeaway

**A rotation is a loop that repeats.** When the ball moves, all five defenders adjust their positions following the defensive tree system. Each defender has a role. Each rotation follows the same pattern. And we repeat it until we get a stop.
