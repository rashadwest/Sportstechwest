# Writing Guide - Book 2: The Decision Crossover
## Picture Book Format (~30 words per page)

**Copyright © 2025 Rashad West. All Rights Reserved.**

---

## FORMAT OVERVIEW

**Format:** Picture Book  
**Words per page:** ~30 words  
**Total pages:** ~10-15 pages  
**Total words:** ~300-450 words  
**Structure:** Each page = 1 scene with ~30 words + illustration

---

## PAGE-BY-PAGE WRITING GUIDE

### PAGE 1: Title Page / Opening
**Words:** ~20-30  
**What to write:**
- Title: "The Decision Crossover"
- Opening line that sets the scene
- Introduce the one-on-one situation

**PROMPT:**
"Write 1-2 sentences (20-30 words) opening with a one-on-one situation. Example: 'Nova faced a defender. She tried to go left. The defender was always there, blocking her path.'"

---

### PAGE 2: The Problem
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Show Nova going the same direction every time
- Show the defender always blocking her
- Create the problem

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova going left every time, but the defender always blocks her. Show the frustration. Example: 'Nova went left. The defender blocked her. She went left again. Blocked again. She was stuck.'"

---

### PAGE 3: Arc Detects
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Arc speaks
- Arc's line: "Decision logic missing. No conditional response."
- Nova's reaction

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Arc detects the problem. Use Arc's exact line: 'Decision logic missing. No conditional response.' Show Nova hearing this."

---

### PAGE 4: Coach's Wisdom
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Coach Circuit speaks
- Coach's line: "Read the defense. If they go left, you go right. That's the logic."
- Nova thinking

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Coach Circuit gives wisdom. Use Coach's exact line: 'Read the defense. If they go left, you go right. That's the logic.'"

---

### PAGE 5: Nova's Decision
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova realizes she needs to read and react
- She decides to learn the crossover
- End of Act I feeling

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova realizing: 'Maybe I need to read and react?' Show her deciding to learn conditional logic."

---

### PAGE 6: Practice Begins
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova practices reading the defense
- She learns: "If defense goes left, then crossover right"
- Show her practicing the crossover

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova practicing reading the defense. Show her learning: 'If defense goes left, then crossover right.' Make it feel like discovery."

---

### PAGE 7: Pattern Recognition
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Arc sees the decision pattern
- Arc explains: "If defender moves left, then crossover right. If defender moves right, then crossover left."
- This is conditional logic

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Arc recognizes the decision pattern. Show Arc explaining: 'If defender moves left, then crossover right.' Show this is conditional logic."

### PAGE 8: The Connection
**Words:** ~30

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) where Nova makes the connection. Show her realizing the crossover is like 'IF [condition] THEN [action].' Use her line: 'This is like if/then code!'"

---

### PAGE 9: Final Challenge
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Final seconds

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) setting up the final challenge. Show the urgency. Show Nova knowing she needs to read the defense and use if/then logic."

---

### PAGE 10: Using the Logic
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova reads the defender's movement
- She uses if/then logic: "IF defender goes left, THEN crossover right"

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing Nova reading the defense. Show her using if/then logic: 'IF defender goes left, THEN crossover right.' Show it working."

---

### PAGE 11: Success!
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova gets past the defender!
- She creates a scoring opportunity
- Show the success

**PROMPT:**
"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) showing the success. Nova crosses over, gets past the defender, creates a scoring opportunity. Show the celebration."

---

### PAGE 12: The Connection
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Nova reflects
- Her line: "Conditional logic works! I can make smart decisions!"

**PROMPT:**

### PAGE 13: Final Message
**Words:** ~30  
**What to write:**
- Final message about decision-making
- Her line: "Coding uses if/then to make decisions, just like basketball."
- End with understanding

"Write 2-3 sentences (30 words) ending the story. Use Nova's line: 'Coding uses if/then to make decisions, just like basketball.' End with her understanding that conditional logic enables smart choices."

## WRITING RULES FOR PICTURE BOOKS

### DO:
- ✅ Keep each page to ~30 words
- ✅ Make each page a complete thought/scene
- ✅ Use simple, clear language
- ✅ Let illustrations tell part of the story
- ✅ Use dialogue to break up text
- ✅ End each page with a moment or action
- ✅ Make it flow from page to page

### DON'T:
- ❌ Don't write more than 30 words per page
- ❌ Don't explain everything - let pictures help
- ❌ Don't use complex sentences
- ❌ Don't put too much on one page
- ❌ Don't forget each page needs to work alone AND flow to next

---

## PAGE STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

**For each page, write:**
1. **Action/Scene** (1-2 sentences)
2. **Dialogue or Thought** (1 sentence, if needed)
3. **Result/Moment** (1 sentence)

**Example:**
- Page 6: "Nova watched the defender. If he moved left, she would go right. That was the logic."
