## Primary thesis

**Sports does not have a data shortage. It has a translation gap.**

Competitive advantage is not simply collecting more data or producing more film. It is turning complex information into clear observations, useful cues, and actions athletes can execute.

**Role name in draft:** Hoop Tendency Experts — players and hybrid staff who see patterns early and can explain them so athletes can act. Analytics are tendencies; numbers are accumulated evidence of those patterns.

Throughline:

> Data becomes information.  
> Information becomes understanding.  
> Understanding becomes action.

### C. Claims requiring cautious / non-settled framing

Do **not** present as universal scientific facts:

- Two clips are always better than any other number
- One cue is always sufficient
- A 12–24 hour feedback window is scientifically optimal
- Athletes always retain feedback best while they can still “feel” the play
- Shorter film sessions always improve performance

Preferred framing:

> The exact number of clips will vary by athlete and situation. The larger principle is to reduce unnecessary information and make the intended action unmistakable.
