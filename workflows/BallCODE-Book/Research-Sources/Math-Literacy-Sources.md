## Primary Sources

### 1. Statistical and Data Literacy in K-12 STEM Education (2024)

**Citation:**
What shapes statistical and data literacy research in K-12 STEM education? A systematic review of metrics and instructional strategies. *STEM Education Journal*, 2024.

**URL:** https://stemeducationjournal.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40594-024-00517-z

**Key Findings:**
- Early engagement with data and statistics in K-12 lays foundational understanding
- Integration of data literacy into math education is crucial
- Statistical thinking should be introduced early
- Real-world data applications enhance learning

**Implications for BallCODE:**
- Integrate data analysis and statistics into episodes from the start
- Use basketball statistics as real-world data
- Build statistical thinking progressively
- Connect math to practical applications

**Relevance to Episodes:**
- Episode 1: Basic statistics (possessions, turnovers)
- Episode 8: Percentages and expected value
- All episodes: Data-driven decision making

### 4. Math Word Problem Readability Research

**Citation:**
Research on readability of math word problems in middle schools. *ERIC*, 2020-2024.

**URL:** https://eric.ed.gov/?ff1=eduMiddle+Schools&q=math+literacy

**Key Findings:**
- Readability of math problems impacts student performance
- Difficult-to-read problems disadvantage students with lower literacy
- Clear, accessible language is essential
- Context matters for understanding

**Implications for BallCODE:**
- Use clear, accessible language in math problems
- Ensure readability matches grade level
- Provide context for math concepts
- Support students with varying literacy levels

**Relevance to Episodes:**
- All episodes: Clear math language
- Exercises: Readable math problems
- Skill Pit-Stops: Accessible explanations

## Key Themes from Research

### 1. Early Data Engagement
- Introduce statistics and data early
- Build foundational understanding
- Use real-world data

### 2. Real-World Applications
- Connect math to practical situations
- Use relevant contexts (basketball)
- Show math in action

### 3. Progressive Development
- Build skills progressively
- Increase complexity appropriately
- Support number sense development

### 4. Accessible Language
- Use clear, readable language
- Match readability to grade level
- Provide context for understanding

### 5. Integration with Other Subjects
- Integrate math with coding and AI
- Show connections between subjects
- Use interdisciplinary approaches
