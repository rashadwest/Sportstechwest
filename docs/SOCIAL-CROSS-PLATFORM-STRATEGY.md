## Operating model: hub and spokes

**YouTube is the home.** Every piece of content is made for YouTube first. X and Facebook are not extra publishing jobs — they are *re-shapes* of work already done. If a platform ever requires net-new content creation, cut the platform, not the sleep.

**The rule that makes this work: never just drop a link.** Every platform downranks outbound links (X and Facebook especially punish YouTube links). Each spoke gets a *native-format* version with a take, and the link goes in a reply/comment or bio.

## One story, four shapes

Each STW News story already produces a script, a hook line, and a vertical video. That's everything the spokes need:

| Platform | Shape | Effort | When |
|----------|-------|--------|------|
| **YouTube** | The Short (made here) | — | Publish day, 16:29 slot |
| **X** | The *take*, text-first: hook line + your one-sentence opinion ("Would you pay for this?" energy). Native clip optional. Link in first reply, not the post | 5 min | Same day, within an hour of the Short |
| **Facebook** | Native video upload of the same Short as a Reel (never a YouTube link) + 2-sentence caption | 5 min | Same day |
| **LinkedIn** | Friday only: best story of the week, business angle | 15 min | Friday (already in weekly plan) |

Weekly extras (choose max one until the streak holds):
- **X Friday thread:** 5 posts — the week's 4 stories + "which one matters most" close. Threads are X's long-form and cost only assembly.
- **Facebook evergreen:** the youth-development / coaching content (10 Tips, systematic thinking) belongs on FB more than news does — parents and coaches live there. One evergreen post/week, native image or video.

## Platform roles (what each is *for*)

- **YouTube** — discovery engine + library. Judged by: streak, CTR, retention.
- **X** — conversation + industry presence. The follower growth comes from *replies*, not posts: 10 minutes/day replying with genuine takes on sportstech/NBA/analytics accounts beats any posting schedule. Judged by: profile visits, replies received.
- **Facebook** — community + evergreen reach. Reels still get outsized organic reach for small pages; coaching/parent groups are where the youth-development content converts. Judged by: Reel reach, shares.
- **LinkedIn** — professional proof-of-work. Judged by: DMs and opportunities, not likes.

## Account hygiene (one-time, 30 min)

- [ ] Same avatar + banner on all four (brand-kit assets)
- [ ] Bios state the same promise: "Where sports and technology crossover. New sportstech news 4x/week." + site link
- [ ] X pinned: best-performing Short or the draft simulator; FB pinned: same
- [ ] Each platform's bio links to YouTube (the hub), not just the website

## Automation path (later, not now)

The dispatcher already ships YouTube on schedule. Phase 2 (after 4 clean weeks manually): extend `stw-iq-dispatcher.py` or n8n to auto-post the X take + FB Reel from each story package (`short.md` already contains the hook line). Manual first — learn what tone lands before automating it.

## What "working" looks like (90 days)

- X: follower count up and, more importantly, replies/quote-posts from sportstech industry accounts
- Facebook: one Reel or evergreen post with 10x typical reach identifies the FB-native content lane
- Cross-platform: YouTube traffic sources in Studio start showing "External — Twitter/Facebook" > 0
