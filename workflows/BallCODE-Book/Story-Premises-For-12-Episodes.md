# Story Premises — BallCODE: Unlocking the Secret Code of Sport and Beyond
Audience: Elementary & Middle school students (grades 3–8); teachers/parents welcome

Twelve self-contained episodes that read like adventures kids want to finish. Each pairs a basketball objective with a coding + math idea and an optional AI mechanic.

---

## 1) The Tip-off Trial — State and Flow
- Setting: Data Court — Center Circle
- Monster: Shadow Press Scouts
- Basketball Objective: Secure tip → first clean possession → controlled transition
- Coding Concept: State (start, live, dead, outcome)
- Math Concept: Basic stats (possessions, turnovers)
- AI Mechanic: Vision cue → detect possession change
- **Foundational AI (Grades 3-5):** AI can see what we see; AI detects changes and asks for confirmation
- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** Computer vision processes visual data; state detection algorithms; confidence scores
- Climax Challenge: Maintain advantage through a 3-step transition sequence without losing state.
- Reflection: What variables mattered most this possession?

## 2) The If/Then Fork in the Key — Conditionals
- Setting: The Key — Painted Lane
- Monster: Turnover Trolls
- Basketball Objective: Beat trap out of PnR
- Coding Concept: If/Else decision tree
- Math Concept: Probability of options
- AI Mechanic: Prompted "coverage advisor" suggests counter
- **Foundational AI (Grades 3-5):** AI helps us choose; AI suggests multiple options with confidence levels
- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** Decision trees evaluate paths; recommendation systems rank options; context weighting
- Climax Challenge: Choose a 6-branch tree path under time.
- Reflection: Which condition flipped your decision?

## 3) Loop of the Rotating Guardians — Loops
- Setting: Perimeter Ramparts
- Monster: Rotation Wraiths
- Basketball Objective: Perfect closeout rotations over multiple passes
- Coding Concept: Loops, invariants, break/continue
- Math Concept: Sequences, time-to-event
- AI Mechanic: Simulate latency penalties
- **Foundational AI (Grades 3-5):** AI knows when we're too slow; AI tracks timing and patterns
- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** Temporal analysis processes time-based data; latency detection; pattern recognition in sequences
- Climax Challenge: Hold rotation invariant across 5 passes.
- Reflection: Where did timing slip and why?

## 4) Pattern of the Phantom Zone — Pattern Recognition
- Setting: The Phantom Zone (2-3 and 1-3-1 illusions)
- Monster: Zone Phantoms
- Basketball Objective: Find seam, collapse, skip
- Coding Concept: Rule-based detectors → patterns
- Math Concept: Similarity/features
- AI Mechanic: Tag features; suggest seam score
- **Foundational AI (Grades 3-5):** AI finds patterns like a detective; AI highlights features and scores opportunities
- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** Feature detection algorithms; pattern recognition systems; machine learning basics
- Climax Challenge: Identify pattern → call best seam play.
- Reflection: Which cues told you the zone's shape?

## 5) The Ledger of Possessions — Data Models

## 8) The Math of the Arc — Percentages/EV

## 9) Algorithm of the Final Play — Algorithms
- Setting: Coach's ATO Lab

- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** Monte Carlo simulation; algorithm comparison; policy optimization; performance metrics

## 12) The AI Oracle — End-to-End System
- Setting: Oracle's Court
- Monster: Uncertainty Chimera
- Basketball Objective: Use assistant safely for live calls
- Coding Concept: AI-in-the-loop with guardrails
- Math Concept: Metrics, calibration
- AI Mechanic: Confidence + "why" output; fallback
- **Foundational AI (Grades 3-5):** Using AI safely and wisely; always check AI suggestions; have backup plans
- **Foundational AI (Grades 6-8):** AI guardrails; confidence thresholds; explainability; fallback systems; human-in-the-loop; AI ethics
- Climax Challenge: Win using assistant with explicit safeguards.
- Reflection: What was the assistant good/bad at and why?
