Strava just acquired Runna — the app that gets runners to the start line with personalized plans for 5K, 10K, half marathon, and marathon.

Strava's the place where millions log runs and share activities; Runna's the one that builds your training plan and keeps you on track. Both stay separate for now — no merged subscription yet — but Strava's betting that helping you train smarter is the next step for its community.

For runners, it means the app you use for tracking and the app you use for planning are now under one roof. For Strava, it's a clear move: more tools for runners, and more reasons to stay in the ecosystem.
