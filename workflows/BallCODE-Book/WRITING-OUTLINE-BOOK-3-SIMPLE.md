## PLOT (3 Acts - Broken into ~13 pages)

### ACT I: The Reactive Defender (~400-500 words)
**What happens:**
- One-on-one situation, defender reacts instantly
- Nova tries simple fakes (one fake left, one fake right)
- Defender doesn't fall for them, reads every move
- Arc detects: "Pattern repetition missing. No loop structure."
- Coach Circuit: "Create a pattern, then break it. That's how loops work—repeat, then change."
- Nova realizes: "Maybe I need to repeat, then break the pattern?"

**End Act I:** Nova decides to learn pattern creation

---

### ACT II: The Pattern Loop (~400-500 words)
**What happens:**
- Defender gets better at reading single moves
- Nova practices in & out dribble
- Learns: "Fake left (repeat), fake left (repeat), fake left (repeat), then go right (break)"
- Arc: "Fake left, fake left, fake left—then go right. That's a loop."
- Math: Sequences - "Repeat 3 times, then break"
- Coding: Loops - "REPEAT [fake left] 3 TIMES, THEN [go right]"
- Nova resists: "Why repeat? Just go the direction I want!"
- Coach: "Patterns create expectation. Breaking them creates opportunity."
- Nova realizes: "This is like a loop—repeat the pattern, then break it!"

**End Act II:** Nova understands loop structures

---

### ACT III: Breaking the Loop (~400-500 words)
**What happens:**
- Final seconds, need to get past defender
- Nova uses loop: REPEAT [fake left] 3 TIMES, THEN [go right]
- Defender falls for the pattern! Commits after 3 fakes
- Nova breaks the loop, goes right, gets past
- Success! Creates scoring opportunity
- Nova: "Loops work! Repeat the pattern, then break it!"
- Connection: "Coding uses loops to repeat patterns, just like deception"

**End Act III:** Nova succeeds using loop patterns
