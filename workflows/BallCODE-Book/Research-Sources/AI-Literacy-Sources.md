## Primary Sources

### 1. Carrisi, M. C., Marras, M., & Vergallo, S. (2025)

**Citation:**
Carrisi, M. C., Marras, M., & Vergallo, S. (2025). A Structured Unplugged Approach for Foundational AI Literacy in Primary Education. *arXiv preprint arXiv:2505.21398*

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.21398

**Key Findings:**
- Unplugged activities (no computers needed) effectively teach AI concepts to elementary students
- Structured approach enhances students' conceptualization, data representation, classification reasoning, and evaluation skills
- Students showed improvements in AI terminology, logical reasoning, and evaluative skills
- Integration into existing curricula is effective

**Implications for BallCODE:**
- Use hands-on, visual activities to teach AI concepts
- Don't require advanced technology for AI literacy
- Structure AI concepts clearly with examples
- Integrate AI naturally into basketball stories

**Relevance to Episodes:**
- Episode 1: AI vision detection (unplugged explanation)
- Episode 2: Decision trees (visual representation)
- Episode 4: Pattern recognition (hands-on activities)
- All episodes: Structured AI concept introduction

### 2. Systematic Review on AI Literacy in K-12 Education (2023)

**Citation:**
Systematic literature review on AI literacy in K-12 education. *STEM Education Journal*, 2023.

**URL:** https://stemeducationjournal.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40594-023-00418-7

**Key Findings:**
- Interdisciplinary approach integrating digital, computational, scientific, and mathematical literacies is essential
- Need for comprehensive AI literacy framework
- Age-appropriate, culturally sensitive pedagogical methodologies are crucial
- Project-based and problem-based learning are effective

**Implications for BallCODE:**
- Integrate AI with math and coding, not as separate subject
- Use project-based learning approach
- Ensure age-appropriate content (grades 3-8)
- Make content culturally sensitive and inclusive

**Relevance to Episodes:**
- All episodes: Interdisciplinary integration
- All episodes: Project-based exercises
- Framework approach: Basketball as vehicle for learning

## Key Themes from Research

### 1. Unplugged Activities
- AI concepts can be taught without computers
- Hands-on activities are effective
- Visual representations help understanding

### 2. Interdisciplinary Integration
- AI should be taught with other subjects
- Integration enhances understanding
- Real-world connections are important

### 3. Age-Appropriate Content
- Content must match developmental stage
- Progressive complexity is essential
- Clear, simple explanations for younger students

### 4. Teacher Support
- Teachers need resources and training
- Clear guides improve confidence
- Professional development is crucial

### 5. Assessment and Measurement
- AI literacy can be measured
- Assessment tools are needed
- Progress tracking is important
